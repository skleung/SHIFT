health++ Fellowship
Project-based opportunities to learn by doing

health++ fellows receive the opportunity to immerse themselves in a quarter-long project tackling high-impact problems in the healthcare sector. Fellows work closely with interdisciplinary teams and an industry or Stanford-affiliated liaison throughout the quarter.

The health++ fellowship is a student initaitive under SHIFT and an opportunity open to all students who want to learn by doing. We want to work with students from any background to find projects that fit your interests!

Fall 2015

Online Hepatitis Tool

About 90% of viral hepatitis deaths are due to complications related to untreated chronic hepatitis B or C. The World Health Organization issued the first guidelines on hepatitis B and C treatment in 2014 and 2015 to develop a comprehensive prevention, diagnosis and treatment action plan; however, most healthcare workers are not trained or familiar with the management and treatment of chronic hepatitis B and C.

Mobile Medical Photography Platform

Physicians often need to document/communicate images of a patient’s physiology . While a smartphone’s camera app is a quick way to address this need,we need a medical-grade mobile photo application that allows for the acquisition, archiving, and viewing of photos along with secure collaboration and hospital integration.

VasaTrac PAD Apple ResearchKit

About 60% of Peripheral Artery Disease (PAD) patients treated using endovascular techniques require re-intervention within 6 months. Considering that patient follow-up after intervention is sporadic, we have limited understanding of how current treatments affect patient activity and whether the failure mode is gradual or abrupt; therefore, continuous monitoring through tools like Apple ResearchKit may be more effective.

Project Kraepelin

Imagine a disease that affects 5% of the population, kills 20% if untreated, has an effective and cheap treatment, yet kills ~500,000 by suicide each year: you have imagined bipolar mood disorder, the single most costly psychiatric disease. Notoriously difficult to diagnose, bipolar disorder has an average diagnosis delay of over a decade while the majority of suicides occur in the first five years of symptoms - modern psychiatry desperately needs a way to diagnose bipolar disorder at its earliest stages.
